# Audio Output

The PGB-1 provides multiple audio output options for different listening situations.

## Output Options

### Internal Speaker

The built-in speaker allows you to hear your music without any external equipment.

- Automatically used when no headphones are connected
- Good for casual listening and sound design
- Limited bass response compared to headphones

### Headphone/Line Output

The 3.5mm stereo jack on the top of the device:

- Connect headphones for private listening
- Connect to mixers, speakers, or audio interfaces
- Automatically switches from internal speaker when connected

!!! warning "Hearing Safety"
    Maximum volume can be very loud, especially with headphones or earbuds. Extended exposure to high volume may damage your hearing.

## Volume Control

### Adjusting Master Volume

Hold ++play++ and press ++up++ or ++down++ to adjust the master output volume.

- Press once for single step changes
- Hold for continuous adjustment
- Applies to both speaker and headphone output

### Track Volume

Each track has its own volume setting in Track Mode (page 4).

## Output Quality

The PGB-1 outputs stereo audio with:

- Full stereo panning support
- All effects processed before output
- Clean signal path

## Connecting to External Equipment

### Headphones

Standard 3.5mm (1/8") headphones work directly:

- Over-ear headphones recommended for best sound
- In-ear monitors also work well
- Avoid very high volumes

### Powered Speakers

Connect to computer speakers or studio monitors:

- Use 3.5mm to 3.5mm cable
- Or 3.5mm to RCA adapter for some speakers
- Adjust PGB-1 volume to appropriate level

### Audio Interface

For recording to your computer:

- Connect to audio interface line input
- Use 3.5mm to 1/4" adapter if needed
- Set PGB-1 volume to avoid clipping

### Mixer

For live performance or complex setups:

- Connect to mixer line or aux input
- Use appropriate adapters
- Set levels to match other sources

## Tips

### Recording Setup

1. Connect PGB-1 to audio interface
2. Set PGB-1 volume to about 75%
3. Adjust interface gain to achieve good levels
4. Record your performance

### Live Performance

1. Connect to PA system or mixer
2. Use a quality cable
3. Do a sound check before performing
4. Have headphones available for monitoring

### Practicing

- Use headphones for focused practice
- The internal speaker works for casual playing
- Consider your environment and volume levels
